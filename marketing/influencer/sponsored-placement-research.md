# Big-Ticket Sponsored Placement — Research & Recommendation

**Goal:** pay ONE big site / blog / channel to create + publish a sponsored video OR blog post that puts Legacy Odyssey in front of 100,000+ relevant, US parenting readers/viewers.

**Prepared for:** Dan — Influencer session — 2026-06-26

**Bottom line up front:** the best *relevant-US-reach per dollar* is NOT the famous big sites (they're enterprise-priced, $10k–50k+ minimums). It's a **US parenting newsletter sponsorship (~$2,000–2,500 to reach 100k inboxes)**, a **dedicated video on a mid-size US mom YouTube channel (~$2,000–6,000, evergreen)**, or a **sponsored post on a Raptive-network mom blog (~$500–2,000, SEO-durable)**. BUT — read the break-even section first. At our current 0.16% site conversion, even perfectly targeted traffic likely loses money on first-year revenue. Fix conversion, then test cheap before going big.

---

## Tier 1 — Micro / mid parenting blogs (10k–100k monthly readers)

Format: sponsored blog post (evergreen, SEO value) + a social share and often a newsletter mention.

| Range | Typical cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Small blog (<10k visitors) | $250–$750 / post | Local/niche publishers; often packaged 3 for $500–$1,500 |
| Established blog (10k+ visitors) | $500–$2,000 / post | Sweet spot for a first real test |
| Larger independent blog (50k–100k) | $2,000–$5,000 / post | Approaches large-site pricing |

- Sponsored blog posts typically run **20–40% cheaper than the same creator's social post**, and they keep driving search traffic for months/years (durable, unlike a feed post). [The Blog Societies](https://theblogsocieties.com/how-to-price-a-blog-post/), [InfluenceFlow](https://influenceflow.io/resources/sponsored-post-rates-complete-2026-pricing-guide-for-influencers-brands/)
- A key insight for us: *"an email list of 5,000 highly engaged subscribers in a niche like parenting can be worth more than 100,000 anonymous monthly visitors."* Engaged niche beats raw pageviews. [InfluenceFlow](https://influenceflow.io/resources/sponsored-post-rates-complete-2026-pricing-guide-for-influencers-brands/)
- **US-audience:** findable per-blog (ask for the media kit / Google Analytics screenshot). Independent US mom blogs are usually 70–90% US — much cleaner than a global mega-site.
- **How to book:** direct (most have a "Work With Me" / "Advertise" page + media kit), or through a network (see Booking Paths).

## Tier 3 — YouTube / video (family & parenting channels)

Format: 60–90 second **integration** inside a normal video, or a **dedicated video** about the product.

**Pricing mechanics.** Every quote reduces to: *recent average views × niche CPM × format multiplier.* [OutlierKit](https://outlierkit.com/resources/youtube-sponsorship-rates/)
- Parenting/lifestyle CPM in 2026: **~$15–$25 per 1,000 views** (integration). [OutlierKit](https://outlierkit.com/resources/youtube-sponsorship-rates/)
- **Dedicated video = 1.3–2x** the integration rate. [OutlierKit](https://outlierkit.com/resources/youtube-sponsorship-rates/)
- So a video that averages **150k views** ≈ 150 × $20 = **~$3,000 integration**, **~$4,500–6,000 dedicated.**

**Named candidate channels (US family/parenting):**

| Channel | Subscribers | Why relevant | Est. sponsorship | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| What's Up Moms | ~4.5M | #1 parenting channel, US moms, practical "Mom Hacks" | Likely enterprise ($10k+); huge reach | [Channel](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCMfXv2enRXepxG92VoxfrEg) |
| Family Fun Pack | ~10.5M | Family vlog, young kids | Enterprise; reach-heavy, less targeted to "new baby" | [TheTilt](https://www.thetilt.com/content-entrepreneur/family-fun-pack-content-business) |
| Daily Bumps | ~5.1M | Young parents, family journey, "Bumps" = baby angle | High; on-brand for new parents | [ThoughtLeaders](https://app.thoughtleaders.io/youtube/daily-bumps) |
| Mama Natural | Mid (natural-birth/pregnancy niche) | **Pregnancy + newborn** — tightest fit to "baby book" buyer | Mid (~$2k–6k dedicated) | Niche, high-intent |
| A 100k–500k-sub US mom channel | 100k–500k | Sweet spot: real reach, still affordable, evergreen | **~$2,000–6,000 dedicated** | Best value tier |

- **Why video is attractive:** a dedicated/integrated video keeps earning views for months — your 100k reach **compounds** rather than expiring like a feed post.
- **US-audience:** check each channel's analytics (ask in the media kit). US mom-vlog channels are typically 50–75% US — verify before paying.
- **How to book:** the big channels have management/MCN contacts (direct email in "About"); mid channels often respond to direct email or are on [Collabstr](https://collabstr.com)/ThoughtLeaders. Macro creators on Collabstr's data run **$2,500–$25,000**; parenting-niche specifically **$800–$4,000 per post**. [Collabstr guide](https://collabstr.com/blog/influencer-marketing-price-guide), [influee](https://influee.co/blog/instagram-influencer-pricing)

- **Motherly's newsletter** reaches a large, very-US, very-relevant audience (the brand is 12M weekly US viewers). A single newsletter send is far cheaper than a full Motherly branded-content program — worth a direct quote.

## RANKED recommendation — best relevant-US-reach per dollar for ~100k+

**#1 — US parenting NEWSLETTER sponsorship (~$2,000–2,500 / 100k inboxes).**
Cheapest path to 100k *opted-in, engaged, US* parents, and the only one with clean click tracking. Start with **Scary Mommy via Paved** (knowable price, no sales call) or a direct **Motherly newsletter** quote. Reach-per-dollar ~$0.02/parent. Most measurable.

**#2 — Dedicated video on a mid-size US mom channel (100k–500k subs, ~$2,000–6,000).**
Evergreen — your reach compounds for months. Pick a **pregnancy/newborn-leaning** channel (e.g., Mama Natural style) so the audience is actually at the baby-book buying moment, not generic family vlog. Video also demos the product better than text. Verify US-audience % first.

**#3 — Sponsored post on a Raptive-network US mom blog (10k–100k, ~$500–2,000).**
Cheapest real test, durable SEO value, easy to get a trackable discount code and a true US-audience read from the blogger directly. Lowest risk to learn whether warm parenting traffic converts for us.

**Explicitly NOT worth it at our stage:** Scary Mommy/Motherly/BabyCenter/The Bump **full branded-content programs**, and the **mega YouTube channels** (What's Up Moms, Family Fun Pack) — all enterprise-priced $10k–50k+ with broad (not new-baby-targeted) audiences. The Bump was already parked here; same logic applies to the whole enterprise branded-content tier. The cheap exception is buying just a single *newsletter send* from one of these names (Tier 4), not a content program.

## The honest caveat — does ANY of this break even?

This is the part that matters. We have to tie it to reality:

- **Cold paid traffic has lost us money.** Google Ads: ~$726 spent, 0 conversions. Meta: ~$1,489 spent, ~3 view-through "conversions" of dubious quality. Lifetime revenue is roughly **$288** across ~7 customers.
- **The site converts ~0.16% of sessions**, with a 9-second average engagement and a /gift page that took 500 sessions to 0 sales. The bottleneck is the **landing page, not the traffic source.**

**Break-even math (conservative, first-year only):**
- Price $29 first year. Minus ~$11 domain (Spaceship) minus ~$1.16 Stripe fee → **~$17 contribution per new customer** in year one.
- A $2,500 newsletter send must therefore produce **~147 paying customers** to break even on first-year revenue.
- From 100k reached: assume a strong **4% click-through** (trusted endorsement) = 4,000 visits. At our **current 0.16%** site conversion = **~6 sales = ~$170 revenue** against $2,500 spent. **A heavy loss.**
- Even at an optimistic **0.75%** conversion on warmer, better-targeted parenting traffic: 4,000 × 0.75% = **30 sales = ~$510**. Still a loss vs $2,500 on first-year revenue.

**What makes it work:**
1. **Lifetime value, not first-year.** Renewals are $49.99/yr. If a customer stays ~3 years, contribution LTV is ~$100+. Break-even on $2,500 then drops to **~25 customers** — achievable from 100k *if* the site converts. LTV is the only way these numbers turn positive, so we must be willing to wait for renewals.
2. **Fix conversion FIRST.** Every dollar of reach is wasted on a 0.16% page. The /gift 0-conversion problem and 9-second engagement are the real leak. A targeted parenting sponsorship is *more* qualified than cold Meta — but it still dead-ends on the same page.
3. **Test cheap before going big.** Do **#3 first** — a single **$500–1,000 mom-blog sponsored post with a unique discount code** — to get a real conversion rate on warm parenting traffic. If that converts even at 0.5–1%, scale into the **$2,500 newsletter**. If it converts like our cold traffic (~0.16%), we've learned it for $750 instead of $2,500, and the problem is confirmed to be the page, not the channel.

**Recommendation in one line:** don't spend $2,500+ on a big placement yet. Spend ~$750 on a trackable US mom-blog sponsored post as a conversion test, fix the landing page in parallel, and only then book the newsletter — judged on **LTV**, not first-year revenue.

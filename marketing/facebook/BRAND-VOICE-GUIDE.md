# Legacy Odyssey — Brand Voice Guide (Facebook + Instagram)

> The reference for how Legacy Odyssey sounds on social. Every post — funny, useful, or
> product — is written against this doc. Applies to Facebook AND Instagram organic posts
> and profiles. Built May 15, 2026 from reference accounts Dan personally selected.

---

## The voice in one line

> **Dry, deadpan, parent-to-parent. The funny is in real moments said with a straight
> face — never zany, never staged, never mean. Cute lives in real photos; wit lives
> in the writing.**

---

## How we landed here

Dan reviewed ~15 brands and 9+ creators. What he rejected matters as much as what he kept:

- ❌ **Comics** (Fowl Language, Boumeries) — telegraph the joke; humor feels worked-for.
- ❌ **Polished product brands** (Babylist, Bobbie, Lalo, MORI) — pleasant, not funny.
- ❌ **Dad and Buried** — too cynical / edgy. We are warm, not bitter.
- ❌ **Cross-category funny brands** (Duolingo, Dollar Shave Club) — too loud, meme-first.

**The four he chose** — and the lane they define:

| Account | Followers | What it contributes |

| **Kidversations** (@kidversations_) | 12.9K | Just quote the funny thing the kid said. The kid is the comedian. |
| **Big Time Adulting** (@bigtimeadulting) | 1.9M | Dry, deadpan, anti-influencer. "Not a lifestyle influencer." |
| **Simon Holland** (@simoncholland) | 761K | Tightest deadpan one-liners. Writing-first. |
| **The Mother Octopus** (@themotheroctopus) | 109K | Warm, self-deprecating, honest oversharing. |

Benchmark that started it: **Henpecked Hal** (@henpecked_hal) — deadpan parenting mini-stories.

## The 5 voice principles

### 1. Deadpan, dry, understated
The humor is in the flat delivery while the situation is absurd. Never zany, never punny,
never exclamation-mark-driven. State the ridiculous thing plainly and stop.

### 2. Writing-first
The funny lives in the words, not a visual gag. The image stays real and simple; the
caption carries the joke. We are not a meme account.

### 3. Observational, not manufactured
Humor comes from a real thing a kid did or a real parenting moment — not a setup-punchline
joke. If it sounds like a comedian wrote it, rewrite it so it sounds like a parent noticed it.

### 4. Anti-influencer — honest and unglamorous
No aspirational, staged, perfect-life content. Real photos, real mess, real moments.
The reader should think "this is my life," not "I wish this were my life."

### 5. Warm, never mean
Dry humor, yes. Bitter, crude, or mocking the reader, no. The parent reading should feel
*seen and understood*, never judged. We skipped Dad and Buried for exactly this reason.

## Narrator

**Working decision: brand-neutral.** The page speaks as Legacy Odyssey ("we"), not as a
named persona. It still sounds like a real parent — just not a specific invented character.
Easier to sustain solo, and avoids the "who is this person" problem.
*(Open to revisit if Dan wants a persona.)*

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Day 4 of being outsmarted by someone who can't say their own name." | "LOL toddlers are SO CRAZY right?! 😜" |
| State the absurd thing flatly, then stop. | Explain the joke or add "#momlife #blessed". |
| Quote the kid word-for-word. The kid is funnier than commentary. | Narrate over the kid or add adult punchlines. |
| Use real, slightly-imperfect photos. | Use staged catalog-style "perfect baby" stock. |
| Let a useful tip just be useful, plainly stated. | Dress a tip up as life-changing magic. |
| Sound like a tired parent who noticed something. | Sound like a brand running a campaign. |

## Positioning rules (the website angle)

- **Always emphasize that the product is a real website.** This is the differentiator people respond to. Use the word *website* (or *site*) often and plainly. "Your baby's book, as a real website at your-childs-name.com." "A password-protected website." "The website that lives at their name."
- **Do NOT claim other baby books "live inside an app" or anything similar.** They don't — most baby books are physical books, scrapbooks, photo albums. That comparison is factually wrong and gets pointed out.
- **Don't position against "apps" as the contrast.** Position FOR the website. *We're a website* lands; *they're an app and we're not* doesn't (and isn't true).

## Banned words & hard rules (apply to EVERY post)

- **"forever" / "permanent"** — never. Product is not marketed on permanence.
- **"Time Vault"** — never. (Section is just "The Vault.")
- **"chapter"** — never. The book has **sections** / **pages**.
- **"family book" / "family's story"** — never. It is a **baby book** about the **child**.
- **No analogies** to a magazine, scrapbook, journal app, or photo album.
- **"delivered instantly"** — never (not accurate).
- **"Your baby's story"** as tagline filler — avoid; describe the product factually.
- **Real/person-sounding domain names** — never. Only ever use `your-childs-name.com`.
- **Real family names** (Eowyn, Ragno, Kate, Roy, Lindsey, Emma, Jeff, Lachlan, Reese,
  Daniel, Jeanine) — never. Generic stand-ins only (Sophia, Sam Smith, etc.).
- **No "founder" language in ads.** (Organic posts: fine to be a small brand, just not "founder pricing".)
- **No family-tree references** — product has no such feature.
- **No face-to-camera video** — Dan is camera-shy. Voiceover / screen-capture / photo posts only.

### Emojis
**Allowed** in social marketing copy — they fit the voice in moderation. Use sparingly and
dry (one, landing like a punchline — not a string). The "no emojis ever" rule applies ONLY
inside the product itself, not to Facebook/Instagram posts.

## Content buckets & cadence

Target **3–5 posts/week**, every post with an image. Rough weekly mix:

| Bucket | Share | Voice | Job |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Funny / relatable** | ~2 per week | Deadpan, observational | Carry engagement (comments, shares) |
| **Useful** | ~1 per week | Plain, helpful, still dry | Carry authority/trust |
| **Product** | ~1 per week | Voice-led, leads with the .com hook | Carry conversion |

- Funny posts are newborn-focused but span all ages — the humor of parenting is universal.
- Useful posts: real newborn/parenting info, plainly stated. Model: clear, no fluff.
- Product posts: always anchored to the proven hook — "Is your baby's name still
  available as a .com?" — never a feature dump.

### The Kidversations format (recurring series — highest-value idea)
"Quote the funny thing your kid said" is a humor format AND a live demo of what the product
does (Legacy Odyssey saves those quotes). Run it as a recurring, named series. It is funny
and it sells without trying.

## Visual direction

- **Real photos, not staged.** Slightly imperfect beats catalog-perfect.
- Cute is carried by the **image**; wit is carried by the **caption**. Don't make the
  image try to be funny.
- Existing dark/cream/gold premium look is fine for product posts; funny/useful posts can
  be simpler and more casual. Consistency of *voice* matters more than consistency of *look*.
- No face-to-camera. Photos of babies/kids/parents, screen-captures, simple text cards.

---

## Quick test before any post goes live

1. Does it sound like a parent *noticed* something — or like a brand *wrote* something?
2. Is the funny in the words, said with a straight face?
3. Real photo, not staged?
4. Zero banned words?
5. Warm — would the reader feel seen, not mocked?

If all five are yes, it's on-voice.

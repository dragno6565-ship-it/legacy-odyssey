## The product in one paragraph

Legacy Odyssey gives a child their own private website at their own **.com** (e.g. your-childs-name.com). Parents build it from an iOS app, Android app, or the web editor, and family visits it in any browser — nothing to install. It starts as a digital baby book but it is **not just a baby book**: it is a record of the child's whole life journey that keeps growing year after year. Live and taking real payments since March 2026.

**The offer & business model:**
- Annual intro: **$29 the first year**, then renews at **$49.99/year**
- Monthly: $4.99/mo + $5.99 one-time setup
- Gift: a $29 gift the recipient redeems for a year plus their own .com

This is a **recurring subscription, not a one-time $29 sale.** The goal is to keep each family for years — ideally through the child's whole childhood — so a customer's **lifetime value is far higher than the first-year price.** Please optimize for acquiring customers we retain for years, not just for the cheapest intro sign-up.

## 1. Brand guide

**Positioning (this is the core message — lead with it):**
> **Not just a baby book. It's your child's whole life journey, on a website of their own.**
Legacy Odyssey tracks a child's whole life journey — every milestone, birthday, holiday, report card, award, artwork, and their lineage — and it stays with them as they grow. The name itself says it: a Legacy Odyssey is a life's journey. Never frame it as only a "first year" or newborn product.

**The unique hook:** the child gets a **real .com domain of their own** — not a username, not a subdomain, not a page inside someone else's app. Family visits it like any website, password-protected.

**Voice & tone:**
- Warm, premium, sentimental — NOT cutesy, NOT baby-talk.
- Speaks to parents as adults making a meaningful decision.
- Short, confident, plain. Say concretely what the product is and what you do with it. Avoid vague, flowery claims.
- Gentle scarcity is fair game: "there's only one your-childs-name.com."

**Visual identity:**
- Colors: warm cream `#faf7f2` (background), near-black `#1a1510` (dark), gold `#c8a96e` (primary CTA/accent), text `#2c2416`.
- Fonts: **Cormorant Garamond** (serif, headlines) + **Jost** (sans, body).
- Overall feel: elegant, premium, warm — like a keepsake, not a tech app.

**Available assets we can share on request:** logo, brand colors/fonts above, business-card designs, product screenshots, a promo video, and a live demo site (your-childs-name.com).

## 2. Target audience

**Primary — new & expecting parents, ~25–40:**
- Just had a baby, or expecting within a few months.
- Sentimental; want to keep and organize their child's memories.
- Comfortable with apps and subscriptions; will pay for something that feels premium and lasting.
- Mostly reached on mobile, often via Instagram/Facebook (assume sound-off — use captions).

**Secondary — grandparents & family buying gifts:**
- Baby-shower gift givers and "I don't know what to get them" buyers.
- The gift path (buy it for someone, they redeem) is a real and growing entry point.

**Tertiary — parents of toddlers (0–3) who haven't documented yet** and feel a little guilty about it. The "not just a baby book / whole life journey" angle speaks directly to them because it isn't too late.

## 3. Main competitors

No competitor gives the child their **own .com** — that is our single biggest differentiator. Everyone else stores memories inside their own app/ecosystem.

| Competitor | What it is | Our edge |
|---|---|---|
| **FamilyAlbum** (most formidable) | Free, unlimited family photo/video sharing app | It's a photo feed inside their app; we're a curated website at the child's own .com |
| **BabyPage** | App → printed physical baby book (iOS only) | Their output is a printed book; ours is a living website |
| **Moment Garden** | Child journal, emails updates to family, optional print books | No custom domain; passive email vs. the child's own site family can visit anytime |
| **BackThen** | Private family journal, privacy-first, "no compression" | Invite-only private app; no public URL, no domain |
| **Keepy** | Artwork/schoolwork keeper with voice/video narration | No public URL or domain; narrower (artwork) focus |
| **23snaps** | Private family photo/video feed ("better than Instagram") | A feed, not a curated book; no domain |
| **BebeMemo** | Smart baby journal with growth tracking | Small footprint; no domain, no narrative book |
| **Notabli** | Calm, anti-algorithm private moment sharing | Tiny/indie; no structure, no domain |

**One-line competitive position:**
> "Every other baby book lives inside an app. Your child's Legacy Odyssey lives at their own .com."

## 4. Locations / regions to target

- **Primary: United States.** This is where our customers convert, where the .com and baby-shower culture fit, and where we want spend focused.
- **Worth testing as secondary:** the US Hispanic audience — the product's site language can be set to Spanish (we also support Hindi). English-speaking Canada is a reasonable extension.
- **Please avoid** broad, low-cost international targeting. We've seen firsthand that cheap non-US traffic does not convert for a US .com product and just inflates numbers. Quality US targeting over cheap reach.

---

## 5. Other brand guidelines & requirements

These are firm — please build them into everything:

- **Lead with "not just a baby book / whole life journey."** Never cap the value at the first year or newborn stage.
- **Assume muted mobile** (Instagram/Facebook) — captions on all video.
- **The mobile app is not required** — the web editor does everything. Don't imply a phone/app is needed.
- **Premium and warm, never cutesy or baby-talk.**

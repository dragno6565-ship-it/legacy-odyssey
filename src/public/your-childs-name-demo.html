Before You Arrived

The days we waited, hoped, and dreamed of you

We found out we were expecting you on a quiet Tuesday morning. The word "pregnant" on that little stick changed everything — the way we looked at the future, the way we looked at each other, the way we looked at the world.

We had been hoping for you for a while. And when you finally arrived — even before you were born — we understood what people meant when they said having a child changes you. It does. You already had.

We spent those nine months imagining what you'd look like. Whether you'd have your father's laugh or your mother's eyes. Whether you'd be quiet or loud, bold or gentle. We didn't know yet. But we already loved who you were going to be.

Chapter Three

Coming Home

The first day everything was different

We buckled you into the car seat for the first time and realized no one had prepared us for this exact moment — the weight of you, the smallness of you, how serious it suddenly felt to drive 25 miles per hour down a road we'd driven a thousand times.

We had decorated your room weeks before. The crib was assembled, the blankets were folded, the nightlight was plugged in. But nothing about the room matched the reality of finally carrying you through the front door.

The dog sniffed you cautiously. The cat kept its distance. Grandma cried. We took a hundred photos and none of them captured it. This is our best attempt to write down what that day felt like — because you deserve to know.

Letters To You

Words written now, meant to be read later

Dear Little One,

You are asleep right now, making small snuffling sounds and taking up most of our bed, and I am sitting here trying to write down what it feels like to be your parent at this exact moment.

You are seven months old. You have four teeth. You love the dog, sweet potato, and bath time. You dislike being put down, loud noises, and the moment we stop bouncing you even slightly. You are the most specific, complicated, wonderful small person we have ever encountered.

I want you to know this: we were not prepared for you. Not really. We thought we were. We had the crib assembled and the books read. But nothing prepares you for the actual weight of a child in your arms, the actual sound of their laugh, the actual way the world rearranges itself around this new gravity.

You rearranged us. We are grateful every single day.

With all the love in the world,
Mom & Dad

Grandma's Banana Bread

From: Grandma Ruth • The recipe that started it all

This recipe has been in the family for three generations. Grandma Ruth made it every Sunday, and the smell of it baking means something to every person who grew up in that house. We want you to know how to make it.

Ingredients: 3 ripe bananas, 1/3 cup melted butter, 3/4 cup sugar, 1 egg (beaten), 1 tsp vanilla, 1 tsp baking soda, pinch of salt, 1.5 cups flour.

Method: Mash bananas with butter. Mix in sugar, egg, and vanilla. Stir in baking soda and salt. Fold in flour. Pour into greased loaf pan. Bake at 350°F for 55-65 minutes. Let cool before slicing (if you can wait).
